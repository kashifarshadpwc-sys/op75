Phase 1 · Weeks 1–4

Foundation

Lock in the schedule. Focus on form, not weight. Add 1–2 reps to your top set each week. Don't increase load until you can hit every set at the listed reps with clean form.

Phase 2 · Weeks 5–8

Build

Add 2.5–5 kg to compound lifts (chest press, lat pulldown, leg press, shoulder press). Add a 4th set to your top exercise per day. Cardio bumps to 20 min on weekdays, 40 min Sat.

Phase 3 · Weeks 9–12

Sharpen

Maintain strength in a deficit. Don't chase PRs — chase form quality. Increase cardio intensity (incline 6, 6 km/h). Last 2 weeks: weigh daily, lock the diet, finish the mission.

Phase 1 · weekly focus
What to do, week by week
  • Week 1 (22–28 Jun): Establish baseline. Walk into every session with the listed weights. Don't add anything. Focus 100% on form — feel where each muscle works. Log every set in the tracker.
  • Week 2 (29 Jun – 5 Jul): Same weights as Week 1. The goal is to own these loads — every rep clean, every set complete. If anything felt sloppy in Week 1, fix it this week.
  • Week 3 (6–12 Jul): Same weights, push for +2 reps on top exercises (so 3×12 instead of 3×10 where shown italic). This tests if your body is ready for the Week 4 bump. If you can't add reps cleanly, stay at 10.
  • Week 4 (13–19 Jul): First load progression. Bump 2.5 kg or 1 plate on compound lifts. Reset reps to 10. If form breaks down with the new weight, drop back to Week 3 load and repeat the week.
Phase 1 · what to expect
Body + scale
  • Energy: First 7–10 days may feel low — your body is adjusting to the calorie deficit + new training stress. Push through; it normalizes.
  • Soreness: Heaviest after legs (Wed). Plan rest, walk Thursday morning, eat protein.
  • Scale: Expect 2–3 kg drop in the first 2 weeks (water + glycogen + early fat). Don't get excited or scared. Trust the trend, not the day.
  • Goal: By 19 July, weigh ~82 kg. That's −3 kg in 4 weeks. Right on track for the Aug 2 milestone of 80 kg.

Phase 2 · weekly focus
What to do, week by week
  • Week 5 (20–26 Jul): Add a 4th set to all lead exercises (chest press, lat pulldown, cable row, leg press, shoulder press, barbell curl). Expect more soreness — volume jump is real. Sat: goblet squat goes up to 10 kg.

  • Week 8 (10–16 Aug): Same weights as Week 7. Push for +1 rep on lead exercises (so 11s instead of 10s). Plank works up to 60 sec. Consolidate.
Phase 2 · what to expect
The middle squeeze
  • Body: Visible muscle hardness starts showing in shoulders and arms. Mirror checks become motivating.
  • Scale: Loss rate slows to ~0.6–0.8 kg/week as your body adapts. This is normal. Don't panic-cut food.
  • Sleep: 4-set days drain you more. Add 30 min to sleep window if possible.
  • Goal: Aug 16 weigh-in target: ~78 kg. That's −7 kg from start. Phase 3 will close the last 3 kg.

Phase 3 · weekly focus
What to do, week by week
  • Week 9 (17–23 Aug): Maintain Week 8 strength loads. Don't chase PRs — preserving strength in a deficit is the goal. Cardio jumps: incline 6, 6 km/h, 22 min on weekdays.
  • Week 10 (24–30 Aug): Same loads. Saturday cardio extends to a full 40 min. This is the volume peak.
  • Week 11 (31 Aug – 6 Sep): Final full-load week. Try +1 rep per set on lead exercises (so 11s instead of 10s). Empty the tank Saturday. This is your last big push.
  • Week 12 (7–13 Sep): DELOAD. Drop 4-set exercises to 3 sets. Same weight, fewer total reps. Cut some accessory loads slightly. WEIGH DAILY this week. Sep 10 = target date — be light, mobile, energized.
Phase 3 · what to expect
The finish line
  • Body: Visible definition in shoulders, chest outline, leaner midsection. Waist will feel noticeably tighter.
  • Scale: Loss rate slows to 0.4–0.6 kg/week. The last 3 kg is the hardest. Don't get discouraged — the trend is what matters.
  • Cravings: Will peak around Week 10. Tactical cheat MEAL (not day) helps reset. Log it. Move on.
  • Goal: Sep 10 weigh-in target: 75 kg. Realistic landing zone: 75–77 kg. Either is a win. Either is transformation.
  • What's next: After Sep 10, transition to maintenance — eat back ~300 kcal, keep training, hold the weight for 4 weeks before considering another cut or a lean bulk.

The one recipe you need

Batch chicken
for the week

Marinate Sunday. One breast a day, ready to slice. Works hot from the air fryer or cold from the fridge over salad.

  1. Buy 7 chicken breasts (~150 g each). Trim fat.
  2. Marinate: 3 tbsp yogurt, 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 1 tsp red chili, 1 tsp cumin powder, ½ tsp turmeric, salt, juice of 1 lemon. Mix, coat all 7. Refrigerate.
  3. Day of: air-fry 1 breast at 200°C for 12–14 min, flipping halfway. Rest 3 min before slicing.
  4. Storage: marinated raw breasts last 3 days in fridge. Days 4–7, freeze Sunday night and thaw morning of.
  5. Variations: swap chili for paprika one day, add black pepper + lemon another. Boredom kills consistency.

Coach's notes.

The plan is the easy part. The hard part is what's not in it — sleep, stress, the weeks when the scale doesn't move. Read this when motivation dips.

Note 01
The 75 kg target is aggressive — own it
  • Realistically you'll lose ~0.8–1 kg/week for the first 3 weeks (water + glycogen + fat), then ~0.5–0.7 kg/week as the body adapts.
  • Total projection: ~8–10 kg in 11 weeks. You'll likely land between 75–77 kg by 10 Sep. That's a win regardless.
  • If you stall for 10+ days, drop 100 kcal from Meal 02 (skip the almonds) and add 5 min cardio. Don't slash food in panic.
Note 02
Sleep is the lift you take in bed
  • Your shift ends ~3 AM. Get to bed by 5 AM, sleep through to 12 PM. That's 7 hours.
  • Blackout curtains, phone in another room, cool temperature. Daytime sleep is harder than night sleep — protect it.
  • If sleep falls below 6 hrs for 3 nights, drop one strength day. Tired lifting injures people.
Note 03
Form before weight. Always.
  • If you can't do 10 clean reps, the weight is too heavy. Drop it.
  • Last 2 reps should be the hardest, not impossible. If reps 1–8 feel like 9–10, you're going too heavy.
  • Ego lifting injures shoulders, knees, lower backs. Your sister can spot you — use her.
Note 04
Cheat days — read this
  • Cheat days are not failure. They're planned. Log every item in the tracker — KFC burger, pizza slice, beef steak, roll.
  • Pick one cheat meal, not a cheat day. KFC burger + diet Pepsi = ~450 kcal. 3 pizza slices = ~860 kcal. One steak meal = ~700 kcal.
  • Eat normally before and after. A cheat meal doesn't wreck the week. A cheat day does.
  • Steak dinner days: skip Meal 02 to make calorie room. Eat steak + veg + 1 small baked potato. That's roughly 700 kcal.
  • Do not skip the gym on cheat days — extra fuel = better workout.
Note 05
What to watch for
  • Energy crash mid-workout: eat Meal 03 closer to gym (1 hr before), add a banana.
  • Sharp joint pain (not muscle soreness): stop that exercise. Swap for a machine version.
  • Sleep less than 5 hrs: skip strength, do 30 min walk instead.
  • Weekly weight up: check water intake, salt, and stool habits before panicking. Trend > day.
Hard rules · do not break
The non-negotiables
  • Six gym sessions a week, Mon–Sat. Saturday is non-negotiable. That's the long cardio.
  • No frozen parathas, no regular soda, no late-night biryani. Three substitutions = a wasted day.
  • Weigh in Sunday morning, log it. Track the trend or lose the plan.
  • If you injure yourself, stop. Pain > pride. We rebuild.
