📖 Mission rules

The plan is the easy part. The hard part is what's not in it — sleep, stress, the weeks when the scale doesn't move. Read this when motivation dips.

Note 01
75 kg is the scale number · athletic-lean is the real goal
  • Realistically you'll lose ~0.8–1 kg/week for the first 3 weeks (water + glycogen + fat), then ~0.5–0.7 kg/week as the body adapts.
  • Total projection: ~8–10 kg in 11 weeks. You'll likely land between 73–76 kg by 10 Sep. Athletic-lean tilt means 72–74 kg with visible muscle definition is the actual win — not just a scale number.
  • If you stall for 10+ days, drop 100 kcal from Meal 02 (skip the almonds) and add 5 min cardio. Don't slash food in panic.
Note 02
Sleep is the lift you take in bed
  • Your shift ends ~3 AM. Get to bed by 5 AM, sleep through to 12 PM. That's 7 hours.
  • Blackout curtains, phone in another room, cool temperature. Daytime sleep is harder than night sleep — protect it.
  • If sleep falls below 6 hrs for 3 nights, drop one strength day. Tired lifting injures people.
Note 03
Form before weight. Always.
  • If you can't do 10 clean reps, the weight is too heavy. Drop it.
  • Last 2 reps should be the hardest, not impossible. If reps 1–8 feel like 9–10, you're going too heavy.
  • Ego lifting injures shoulders, knees, lower backs. Your sister can spot you — use her.
Note 04
Cheat days — read this
  • Cheat days are not failure. They're planned. Log every item in the tracker — KFC burger, pizza slice, beef steak, roll.
  • Pick one cheat meal, not a cheat day. KFC burger + diet Pepsi = ~450 kcal. 3 pizza slices = ~860 kcal. One steak meal = ~700 kcal.
  • Eat normally before and after. A cheat meal doesn't wreck the week. A cheat day does.
  • Steak dinner days: skip Meal 02 to make calorie room. Eat steak + veg + 1 small baked potato. That's roughly 700 kcal.
  • Do not skip the gym on cheat days — extra fuel = better workout.
Note 05
What to watch for
  • Energy crash mid-workout: eat Meal 03 closer to gym (1 hr before), add a banana.
  • Sharp joint pain (not muscle soreness): stop that exercise. Swap for a machine version.
  • Sleep less than 5 hrs: skip strength, do 30 min walk instead.
  • Weekly weight up: check water intake, salt, and stool habits before panicking. Trend > day.
Hard rules · do not break
The non-negotiables
  • Six gym sessions a week, Mon–Sat. Saturday is non-negotiable. That's the long cardio.
  • No frozen parathas, no regular soda, no late-night biryani. Three substitutions = a wasted day.
  • Weigh in Sunday morning, log it. Track the trend or lose the plan.
  • If you injure yourself, stop. Pain > pride. We rebuild.

Foundation

Lock in the schedule. Focus on form, not weight. Add 1–2 reps to your top set each week. Don't increase load until you can hit every set at the listed reps with clean form.

Build

Phase 1 · weekly focus
What to do, week by week
  • Week 1 (29 Jun – 5 Jul): Establish baseline. Walk into every session with the listed weights. Don't add anything. Focus 100% on form — feel where each muscle works. Log every set in the tracker.
  • Week 2 (6 – 12 Jul): Same weights as Week 1. The goal is to own these loads — every rep clean, every set complete. If anything felt sloppy in Week 1, fix it this week.
  • Week 3 (13 – 19 Jul): Same weights, push for +2 reps on top exercises (so 3×12 instead of 3×10 where shown italic). This tests if your body is ready for the Week 4 bump. If you can't add reps cleanly, stay at 10.
  • Week 4 (20 – 26 Jul): First load progression. Bump 2.5 kg or 1 plate on compound lifts. Reset reps to 10. If form breaks down with the new weight, drop back to Week 3 load and repeat the week.
Phase 1 · what to expect
Body + scale
  • Energy: First 7–10 days may feel low — your body is adjusting to the calorie deficit + new training stress. Push through; it normalizes.
  • Soreness: Heaviest after legs (Wed). Plan rest, walk Thursday morning, eat protein.
  • Scale: Expect 2–3 kg drop in the first 2 weeks (water + glycogen + early fat). Don't get excited or scared. Trust the trend, not the day.
  • Goal: By 26 July, weigh ~82 kg. That's −3 kg in 4 weeks. Right on track for the 9 Aug milestone of 80 kg.

once a week

Sunday afternoon. One session. Sets you up for the entire week. Athletic-lean tilt means one big chicken breast (250g) per day — front-loaded protein.

  1. Batch boil 6–8 eggs — bring to boil, simmer 10 min, ice bath, peel half (rest peel-as-you-go). Keep in fridge 5–7 days. 2 daily for Meal 4 post-workout.
  2. Marinate 5–6 chicken breasts (~250 g each) — 3 tbsp yogurt, 1 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 1 tsp red chili, 1 tsp cumin, ½ tsp turmeric, salt, juice of 1 lemon. Coat all. Refrigerate.
  3. Day of: air-fry 1 breast at 200°C for 14–16 min (250 g needs longer than 150 g), flipping halfway. Internal temp ≥ 74°C. Rest 3 min, then slice for Meal 3.
  4. Storage: marinated raw breasts last 3 days fridge / 7 days freezer. Thaw morning of.
  5. Whey shake daily (Meal 4) — Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard, 1 scoop + 250 ml whole milk + ice. Shake immediately post-gym at 3 AM. One 5lb/2.27kg tub lasts ~2.5 months at 1 scoop/day. Verify current Pakistani retailer & pricing before buying — protein-supplement market shifts often.
  6. Variations: swap chili for paprika one day, add black pepper + lemon another. Boredom kills consistency.
